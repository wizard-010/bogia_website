State to pay K6m for Manam Resettlement

Share

Source: The National
07th December, 2024

A COURT has ordered State to pay K6 million within six months for the permanent resettlement of displaced Manam Islanders after 30 years or the Finance secretary will have to explain.

More than 30,000 Manam Islanders have been displaced after the 1996 and 2004 volcanic eruptions.

Madang resident judge Vergil Narakobi, who presided the case in the National Court in Madang, ordered that the K6million should be made available within six months for the resettlement exercise to take place or the Finance secretary would be appearing in the National Court in Madang on March 3, 2025 to provide an explanation on the funding.

“I will order that the State pays for the plaintiff’s costs to be taxed as it is also appropriate that the State pays for the second and fourth defendant’s costs since the State showed no interest in this very important proceeding, affecting lives of many vulnerable citizens of Papua New Guinea,” he ordered.

The plaintiff in this case is Bruce Sila for himself and 10,813 other displaced Manam Islanders against the State and others concerning the continuous neglect and breach of basic human rights.

Chairman of Manam Resettlement Authority (Mara) is the second defendant, Madang Provincial Government as the third and Manam Resettlement Authority as the fourth defendant.

Narakobi said that it was declared that the defendants have jointly or severely breached their duties to the displaced Manam Islanders as required under the Manam Resettlement Authority Acts of 2006 and 2016 respectfully.

“It is declared that the defendants’ lack of provision of basic government services to the plaintiffs including education, health services and safe water supply within or around the care centres on Bogia mainland since their temporarily resettlement in 2004 up to the present day is a breach of their human rights under Section 36 (1) and Section 37 (1) of the Constitution,” he ordered.

He further ordered that to prevent future breaches of the displaced Manam Islanders rights, the defendants shall give effects to Section 4 (a) of the Mara Act 2016 by immediately commencing the process of identifying suitable land for compulsory acquisition to enable permanent resettlement of the displaced Manam Islanders and commit to completing the process of compulsory acquisition by Sept 3, 2025.

“It is also surprising that the funds made available for the Manam resettlement exercise have not been audited which the evidence of Dr Boga Figa as the authority chairman does not show that the finances of the authority have been audited by the Auditor-General so therefore he is ordered by this court to perform the statutory functions of the authority,” he ordered.

Narakobi also ordered that the defendants enter into a memorandum of agreement with Royal PNG Constabulary to provide policing to all the care centres in Mangem, Asuramba and Potsadam until permanent resettlement and adequate policing in Manam and Boisa Islands.
